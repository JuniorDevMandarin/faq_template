About Web School Area

Where can I see the Classroom assessments?
In "My Classrooms" you can see all the classes that have been created under the same teacher's account. Here you can see how each class has progressed and what educational topics they are learning together.
How can I see the progress of the students in class?
Inside the Classrooms Area you can find the students progress by clicking on the name of each class. There you can see the progress chart of each of your students and it will show you how they are progressing in real-time and thus offer personalized support to each of them during the class.
How can I know the educational content for each minicourse?
In the Mini-courses section you can find a list of short courses so that you can choose the ones that interest you, according to the concepts of Computational Thinking and gaming platform. Each minicourse has a description of what you will learn and the academic objectives you will accomplish by completing it. It also indicates what grade level it is designed for and what gaming platforms you can find it on.
How do I know the level/age of each mini-course?
Our courses are divided into three groups: 1A, 1B, and 2. To know which school course is appropriate for you, we recommend following the references that group our courses. Group 1A is designed for students between 5 and 7 years old, group 1B is for students between 8 and 11 years old, and group 2 is for students between 11 and 14 years old.
What mini-course can I choose for my students?
To choose a mini-course, it is important to take into account their grade level, the devices needed to complete it, and the relationships it has with other mini-courses. In our learning experiences, we always include tutorial videos and games on platforms such as Roblox, Minecraft, Web, among others. To make your search easier, we recommend filtering the courses by grade and the concepts you wish to learn.
Where can I find which concepts apply to the Mini-course?
The educational concepts learned in each mini-course are available below the title of each one. Additionally, you can see the academic standards that each course meets in the standards section, located on the right side of each Mini-course description.
How long does it take to complete a Mini-course?
Most Mini-courses are designed to be completed in a 1-hour class. The average execution time is located in the header of the Mini-course.
What is a CSTA?
CSTA stands for Computer Science Teachers Association, which is an American teachers' association that promotes computer science education for elementary and secondary school students (between 4 and 19 years old). This association has created a curriculum to help teachers teach computer science in the classroom. The CSTA curriculum covers many topics and concepts, such as programming, artificial intelligence, robotics, and online safety.

About Roblox

What devices can I use to access Roblox?
Roblox can be used on PC Windows, Mac, iOS, Android, and iPad devices.
How can I download Roblox?
To download Roblox, the process may differ depending on the device you're using. Here are the instructions for each device:

  1. PC Windows, Mac: Open your web browser and search for Roblox. From there, you'll be able to download the desktop version.
  2. Android/Tablet Devices: You can download the application directly from Android Apps on Google Play.
  3. iPad: You can download the application directly from the App Store.
If you need further assistance, we offer a Live Chat service, WhatsApp Web, and email support at schools@minicoder.com.
How to access Roblox school experiences?
To access Minicoders' gaming experiences, you must first log in with the credentials provided by the Minicoders Schools team. Then, you must enter the private Minicoders Schools 21 group where you can find all the gaming experiences we offer on Roblox. There you will find a Private Server for each Gaming Experience. Once inside the server, you can start your class!

Please keep in mind that students will not be able to join the server if there is no teacher present.
I can’t login in Roblox due to incorrect username or password
Firstly, make sure that you have entered your username and password correctly. Remember that Minicoders passwords are generated by a combination of capital letters and numbers.

If you can't remember your login details or encounter any issues logging in, you can choose the following options:
  1. Check the list of usernames and passwords in the student tables in your School Area
  2. We suggest using one of the extra student accounts available for emergency use and report all users who haven't been able to access Roblox.
What should I do if I can't login in Roblox?
If a user is unable to log in to Roblox even after solving the verification captcha, please contact operations@minicoders to report the issue.
Users can’t log in Roblox due to Captcha issues
Roblox uses a captcha verification to prevent bot users. The captcha process typically functions as a pattern recognition model. You need to follow the captcha request. However, if you have more questions or concerns, please check our explanatory video.
iPad or PC lags (slow or does not show all elements) in Roblox.
If you experience lagging while playing Roblox on your device and want to improve performance, you can adjust the graphic details manually. Please to adjust that, follow these steps:

  1. Click on the Roblox logo in the upper left corner of the screen.
  2. Select the Settings tab.
  3. Choose Graphic detail and set the mode to Manual.
  4. Look for the row that contains quality adjustments (usually the 7th row).
  5. Use the graphics quality bar to decrease the quality to the minimum.

By adjusting the graphic details in Roblox settings, you can ensure smoother gameplay on your device.
Why are there students who have been removed from the game and can't enter Roblox again?
Roblox has communication and platform usage policies that must be followed. If any of these policies are violated, the student's account may be temporarily blocked. To learn more about these policies, you can visit the Minicoders' guide to using experiences.
What should I do if an error appears in Roblox?
At Minicoders, we offer a guide to common errors that can help you solve some technical problems on the spot. In case you need external assistance, we have a Live Chat service, WhatsApp Web, or our email account schools@minicoder.com available.
Roblox Error 6
There is an issue with the internet connection. It is possible that your connection has dropped or the antivirus is blocking Roblox. Check your internet connection and make sure it is stable and working properly.

If the problem persists, check if your antivirus is blocking Roblox as a threat or contact schools@minicoders.com
Roblox Error 264
The same account started the game from a different device. That means you have opened two different clients with the same account. To solve this, simply close one of the two clients you have opened
Roblox Error 268
If you encounter error 268 on your screen, it indicates that the server has kicked you out. You have been expelled because of unexpected client behavior. This can happen if Roblox conflicts with an exploit program that you have installed. The solution is to uninstall the conflicting program, try to restart the application by either deleting and re-downloading it or simply shutting it down and reopening it.
Roblox Error 271
Sometimes, you may encounter this error when the server has no active player or for an unknown reason. If you encounter this error, please try to rejoin the game.
Roblox Error 272
Lost connection due to an error. This could be because Roblox has detected an exploit that you need to close. An exploit is a computer program, a part of software, or a script that takes advantage of an error or vulnerability to cause unintended or unexpected behavior on software, hardware, or any electronic device.
Roblox Error 273
Disconnected from the game. This may be due to receiving a warning, attempting to open the same account from another device, being banned, or having an unstable internet connection.
Roblox Error 280
Your current version of Roblox is outdated. Please update Roblox and try again.
Roblox Error 524
You are not authorized to access this game or do not have permissions. To review this error please contact Minicoders customer support. We offer real-time assistance through WhatsApp Web, or you can send us an email at schools@minicoders.com. Our support team will be happy to assist you in resolving the issue.
Does Minicoders allow communication between players in Roblox?
With Minicoders Schools experiences, communication between players only occurs among students in the same class. To ensure that communication and collaboration only take place among students, Minicoders works with exclusive private servers for each class. Furthermore, this communication can occur under the privacy policy of Roblox, which is approved by COPPA and the FTC, meaning that it is a company that cares deeply about the safety of children in all of its experiences.
Can students use Minicoders Schools experiences outside of school hours?
No, Minicoders Schools does not permit access to gaming experiences without the presence of a teacher or account administrator.
